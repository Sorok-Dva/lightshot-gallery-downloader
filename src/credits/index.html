Lightshot Gallery Downloader

A modern Manifest V3 extension crafted to retrieve every screenshot from your Lightshot gallery in one secure, streamlined workflow.

Author: Сорок два (SorokDva) — developer & product designer focused on privacy-first tooling.

Release: Built in 2017 and refactored in 2025 with TypeScript, TailwindCSS, and a resilient download pipeline.

Mission: Help Lightshot users export their galleries without hitting throttling limits or losing files.

What’s new in this refactor

Robust pipeline

Sequential mode with smart throttling, exponential backoff, and per-file logging keep downloads stable even on huge libraries.

Streamlined interface

A Tailwind-powered control panel embeds directly into Lightshot’s gallery, exposing real-time progress and quick recovery actions.

Privacy-conscious

No external analytics, no background scraping—downloads happen locally with your Lightshot session cookies only.

Open for contributors

Source code lives on GitHub. Issues, ideas, and pull requests are welcome to keep the tool sharp and dependable.

Next chapter: ScreenMe

Lightshot popularised instant sharing, but its public-by-default model leaves screenshots discoverable by anyone who guesses a URL. I built ScreenMe as a privacy-centric successor.

  • Zero-leak visibility

    Private captures stay encrypted at rest; URLs cannot be brute-forced or sniffed. Only the owner can access private shots.

  • Modern sharing flows

    Generate expiring links, delegate access on a per-project basis, and audit who viewed your content.

  • Security-first architecture

    ScreenMe pairs end-to-end encryption with regional data storage so your screenshots never leak in transit.

  • Roadmap

    Desktop capture apps, instant annotations, and an API for teams are currently in development.
